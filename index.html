The coding chemist?

As a non-binary person with a BSc and PhD in chemistry, I had a difficult time trying to figure out how I fit into the academic world. I learned computer modeling of chemistry and honed my teaching skills, but it was still a challenge to find a place where I felt I belonged. I turned to coding after dealing with some severe burnout, requiring more flexibility and creativity in my work.

Now, I specialize in designing and coding websites and other online tools for science research and education. I also take on non-scientific projects that ignite my passion for social justice and environmentalism. I went into science to make the world a better place, and I can now do so on a global scale via web development.

Well-Organized HTML and CSS

I write clean and well-formatted HTML5, ensuring that the code is easy to read, understand, and trouble shoot. I stay informed regarding the latest industry best practices.

Solo Designer or Collaborative Coder

While I enjoy designing websites and then coding them, I can also take a designer's PSD and accurately convert it into a webpage. I am happy working solo or collaborating with others.

The Importance of Science

Because of my scientific background, I can design and code very technical websites or help make technical content more accessible to a wider audience.
